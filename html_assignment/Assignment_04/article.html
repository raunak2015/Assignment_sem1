History of Computers and the Internet

Early Computers

The history of computers began with mechanical devices such as the abacus. Later, Charles Babbage proposed the Analytical Engine, considered the first concept of a programmable computer. These early machines laid the foundation for modern computing. Innovation was slow but steady. Mechanical computation evolved into electronic systems. CPU became the brain of computers. The difference engine was a milestone. Fun fact: Ada Lovelace is considered the first programmer.

Generations of Computers

The CPU has evolved from vacuum tubes to modern microprocessors. The 1st generation used vacuum tubes, while the 2nd relied on transistors. The 3rd introduced integrated circuits. By the 4th generation, computers became personal and affordable. The 5th generation focuses on artificial intelligence. Punch cards Graphical interfaces replaced old input methods Moore's Law predicted rapid growth Binary logic became essential.

Birth of the Internet

The WWW was invented by Tim Berners-Lee. It revolutionized communication. The power of the Web is in its universality.

“Access by everyone regardless of disability is an essential aspect.” – Tim Berners-Lee
The internet connected the world Ctrl + C became universal shortcuts. Text copied to clipboard. console.log('Hello Internet'); was the start of many journeys.
